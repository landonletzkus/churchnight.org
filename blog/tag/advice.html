SHOULD YOU MARRY FOR LOVE OR MONEY?

August 06, 2020  /  Church Night

I’ve never told anyone this before so I might as well spill the beans to the entire internet right here right now on this famous blog - I’ve been married before. A few times. Yes I know I give off a “sacrificial virgin” appearance, but this innocent little youth minister’s blood couldn’t even purify a magic eraser. Know what I mean? Me neither. Because I’ve been through it all when it comes to marriage, I want to offer a little advice for those of you thinking about what some people call “holy matrimony” and what I call “a huge mistake.” The biggest question I get asked by many of my teen sunday schoolers thinking about marriage is “Kathy, should I marry for love or for money?” While I think the answer is obvious, let’s break down the pros and cons of each scenario and you can decide at the end for your$elf.

Marrying for love

Pros:

  • Marriage will be really fun for at least a few years before slipping into an inevitable pit of despair

  • Way less lying

  • You can get ugly without concern

Cons:

  • Cheating is harder on you emotionally

  • Weird Netflix rules and protocols

  • Nearly impossible to acquire wealth because money means less when you’re “rich in love” or whatever

Marrying for money

Pros:

  • You’re rich

  • You can live a life of luxury and travel the world to exotic places like Cancun (shout out to Senor Frogs!)

  • OK to cheat

  • Don’t have to consider your partner’s feelings

  • Good presents

  • Air conditioning

  • You get to do the “white glove” test after your maid leaves and if you find any dust, you get to throw a huge fit

  • Easier to get away with crimes

  • A vast network of other rich friends that will ensure your wealth stays with you and your family for generations

Cons:

  • Labor unions

  • No other cons

OK folks, that about sums up every single pro and con I can think of when it comes to marrying for love or for money. I hope it helps you in your decision! Please let me know in the comments which one you decided on! Right now I need to go see if my alimony check has come in the mail yet, so I hope to catch you next time when we will all surely learn a little bit more about how to stay devoted to faith.

Blessings and Light,

Kathy
